Since we're all about keyword ciphers lately, here is one with a twist. *Both* alphabets have been mixed with a keyword.
Usually, only the ciphertext alphabet is mixed, like this:

pt: abcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwxyz
ct: KEYWORDABCFGHIJLMNPQSTUVXZ

where a → K, b → E, etc.

But now, both; for example,

pt: firstkeyzabcdghjlmnopquvwx
ct: SECONDKYABFGHIJLMPQRTUVWXZ

so that f → S, i → E, etc.

Here is a ciphertext. The task is not to find the plaintext, but to find the TWO keywords. Have fun.
