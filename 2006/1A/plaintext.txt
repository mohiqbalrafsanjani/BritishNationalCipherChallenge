by lord st. vincent, admiral of the blue and commander in chief
of his majesty's ships and vessels employed and to be employed
in the mediterranean, etc., etc., etc.,

you are hereby required and directed to proceed aboard the minerve
and to make way with utmost expedition off gibraltar to seek out
the spanish fleet. you are to carry intelligence concerning their
disposition to the british fleet off st. vincent, making rendezvous
with the victory on or around february thirteenth. martin will
accompany you and he is directed to spend his time deciphering and
interpreting the enclosed pages from marlowe's diary - they seem
only to be lightly encrypted. babbington is the key.

given on board the victory, off cape st vincent,
qeventh january qeventeen linety qeven
to commodore horatio nelson, esqr
by command of the admiral sir john jervis
